# 8. Section 11.1 — Answering the Research Questions

## 8.1 Main Research Question

The approved main question is:

```text
How can irregular ICU time-series data be converted into clinically
interpretable proxy-state representations and used, under explicit causal
assumptions, to support DAG-guided adjusted effect estimation for
in-hospital mortality?
```

Answer it directly.

The answer should state that the thesis demonstrates an evidence-tracked methodological framework in which:

1. dataset-specific irregular measurements are converted into normalized data contracts;
2. clinically inspired rule-based proxy states are created;
3. irregular-time-series models predict the rule-derived proxy states;
4. prediction outputs are normalized and algorithmically aggregated;
5. project-specified DAGs define adjustment logic;
6. matching and heterogeneous-effect estimators analyze selected proxy-state exposures;
7. robustness and provenance checks qualify the findings.

State that the framework was implemented and empirically exercised across both MIMIC-III and PhysioNet.

Immediately qualify the answer:

* proxy states are not chart-adjudicated diagnoses;
* the exposures are often illness-state constructs rather than well-defined interventions;
* DAG correctness is assumed rather than empirically established;
* adjusted effect estimates remain conditional on identification, measurement, overlap, and model assumptions;
* the pipeline does not produce clinical treatment recommendations.

A suitable high-level conclusion is:

```text
The thesis demonstrates the feasibility of linking irregular-time-series
representation, proxy-state prediction, and assumption-guided adjusted
effect estimation in one auditable workflow, while showing that the
strength of the resulting causal interpretation is constrained chiefly
by construct validity, intervention definition, causal-graph validity,
overlap, and provenance.
```

Do not describe the main question as conclusively solved.

# 22. Construct and Measurement Validity

Address:

## 22.1 Proxy-state validity

* clinically inspired rules are not chart-adjudicated diagnoses;
* thresholds may be incomplete approximations of clinical definitions;
* dataset-specific proxies are not perfectly equivalent;
* predicted and majority-vote proxy states add further classification error;
* shared systematic error may survive majority voting.

## 22.2 Exposure measurement

* binary exposure states can compress severity, duration, and timing;
* proxy onset relative to outcome and covariates may be ambiguous;
* exposure misclassification can bias both predictive and causal analyses.

## 22.3 Outcome measurement

* the outcome is processed `in_hospital_mortality`;
* outcome construction is implementation supported but producing-artifact provenance is incomplete;
* no competing-risk or post-discharge mortality analysis was performed.

## 22.4 Missingness and observation process

* measurement frequency may reflect clinical concern and care processes;
* preprocessing and model representations may not remove informative observation bias;
* missingness handling is not equivalent to causal adjustment for measurement decisions.

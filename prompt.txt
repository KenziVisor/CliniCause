# 7. Expand the Causal-Assumptions Table

Update:

```latex
\label{tab:causal-assumptions}
```

Retain the existing assumptions and add concise rows for:

## Proxy-state measurement

The binary exposure is a rule-derived, predicted, or majority-vote proxy state and may contain construct and classification error.

## Outcome measurement

`in_hospital_mortality` must be consistently and correctly constructed from the processed outcome artifact.

## Temporal ordering

Adjustment variables must be measured or conceptually occur before the relevant proxy-state exposure contrast. Repository graph ordering does not independently prove temporal ordering in patient records.

## Nuisance- and effect-model adequacy

LinearDML and CausalForestDML require sufficiently adequate nuisance and effect models, regularity conditions, and appropriate cross-fitting behavior for their intended interpretation.

## No interference

One record’s proxy-state exposure should not affect another record’s outcome under the working unit-level interpretation.

## Sampling and target population

Original and outcome-downsampled analyses correspond to different empirical populations unless reweighting or another transport argument is supplied.

For every row, distinguish:

* methodological requirement;
* repository support;
* unresolved limitation.

Do not state that any assumption has been verified merely because it is represented in source code.

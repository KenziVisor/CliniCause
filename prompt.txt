## 6.1 Resource utility and reuse

Present the primary contribution positively.

Explain that the constructed resources let researchers:

* compare alternative causal estimators;
* test new irregular-time-series representations;
* refine or replace proxy-state definitions;
* substitute DAGs and adjustment sets;
* study cohort and support sensitivity;
* audit construct and artifact lineage;
* reuse estimator-ready tables without rebuilding every integration stage.

Emphasize that CliniCause contributes reusable analytical resources, not merely final effect tables.

## 6.2 Cross-dataset portability

Interpret the cross-dataset findings:

* the same construction and evaluation sequence operated across two heterogeneous ICU sources;
* predictive leadership differed by dataset;
* effect magnitudes and rankings differed;
* similarly named proxy states are not assumed to be semantically identical;
* results remained dataset specific and unpooled;
* portability is demonstrated at the workflow and interface level.

Frame preservation of dataset-specific definitions as a design strength.

Do not claim pooled clinical replication.

## 6.3 Estimator triangulation and CausalPFN

Discuss the estimator evidence prominently:

* complete 19/19 directional agreement between CausalForestDML and LinearDML;
* 18/19 agreement across both DML estimators and CausalPFN;
* the value of triangulation across different model forms;
* CausalPFN’s promise as a complementary estimator for these resources;
* the PhysioNet-shock disagreement as useful model-sensitivity evidence.

The Discussion must not reduce CausalPFN to a footnote.

Use one bounded statement explaining that its archived estimator-specific sensitivity and permutation package is less complete than the DML package.

Explain that directional agreement:

* supports robustness of the observed sign pattern within the implemented design;
* does not establish equal magnitudes;
* does not by itself prove causal identification.

Do not add unsupported CausalPFN architecture, training, theory, novelty, or citation claims.

---

## 6.4 LLM-assisted design and future research

Present the LLM component as:

* a structured design-time aid;
* a way to translate domain and causal reasoning into inspectable proposals;
* separated from deterministic patient-level execution;
* subordinate to project/human selection and encoded source authority.

Discuss future work such as:

* clinician evaluation of proxy constructs;
* multi-LLM or multi-expert comparison;
* isolated LLM-design ablation;
* alternative proxy ontologies;
* alternative DAGs and adjustment sets;
* stronger uncertainty and repeated-run evaluation;
* richer CausalPFN diagnostics;
* external validation on additional datasets.

Do not claim that the LLM validated clinical constructs or estimated patient-level effects.

---

## 6.5 Centralized limitations

Use one concentrated limitations passage or subsection. Do not scatter defensive language throughout the Discussion.

Prioritize:

1. Proxy states are transparent analytical constructs, not chart-adjudicated phenotypes.
2. DAGs encode project assumptions and do not prove intervention validity.
3. Observational analyses remain vulnerable to unmeasured confounding and unresolved identification assumptions.
4. Repeated-run uncertainty and diagnostic coverage are incomplete for some archived comparisons.
5. CausalPFN has a smaller archived diagnostic envelope than the DML estimators.
6. Exact historical producing revisions, configurations, and checkpoint/export lineage remain incomplete.
7. Current-runtime and anonymous-release status remain separately gated.
8. Dataset access restrictions and lack of external clinical validation limit immediate generalization.
9. The LLM design layer lacks an isolated ablation.

Pair major limitations with the resource feature or next experiment that can address them.

Example pattern:

```text
Although the current proxy states are not chart-adjudicated phenotypes,
their deterministic definitions and preserved lineage make them directly
replaceable and testable in future clinical validation studies.
```

Maintain an optimistic, technically precise tone.

# Conclusion

Write exactly one compact paragraph.

It should state:

* what CliniCause constructs;
* that it spans MIMIC-III and PhysioNet 2012;
* that structural, cohort, provenance, and analytical validation support reuse;
* that broad agreement across DML and CausalPFN demonstrates the value of estimator triangulation;
* what research agenda the resources enable.

Do not introduce:

* a new number;
* a new citation;
* a new experiment;
* a public-release claim;
* a clinical recommendation.

Use a release-neutral closing sentence. For example:

```text
By providing transparent, estimator-ready resources rather than only final
effect tables, CliniCause establishes a reusable foundation for testing new
proxy definitions, temporal representations, graph assumptions, and causal
estimators in irregular clinical data.
```

Revise this wording as needed, but preserve its bounded meaning.

# Quality Rules

## Evidence rules

* Every path must be repository-relative where possible.
* Exact external absolute paths may be recorded only as provenance facts.
* Every numerical-result candidate must identify an exact source artifact.
* Every unfamiliar schema must be checked against source code.
* Every execution claim must have result, log, or run-summary support.
* Every missing stage must be classified as failed, skipped, absent, or unclear.
* Every thesis-suitable figure or table must have a provenance chain.
* Every high-risk causal or clinical claim must remain qualified.

## Interpretation rules

Maintain strict distinctions between:

* implementation and execution;
* prediction and proxy construction;
* proxy states and diagnoses;
* association and adjustment;
* matching and model-based CATE;
* CATE and ATE;
* estimator output and clinically actionable effect;
* robustness diagnostics and proof of causal identification;
* missing diagnostics and evidence of no effect.

## CSV rules

* Use UTF-8.
* Use a single header row.
* Quote fields when necessary.
* Keep one semantic record per row.
* Do not place Markdown inside CSV cells.
* Use repository-relative paths where possible.
* Use consistent controlled vocabulary for status fields.
* Preserve exact numerical strings only when copied from a verified artifact.
* Do not invent empty values; leave blank or use an explicit audit marker.

## Markdown rules

* Use concise headings.
* Prefer structured tables for inventories.
* Include repository-relative paths in backticks.
* Clearly separate verified facts, interpretations, and unresolved issues.
* Avoid thesis-style narrative.
* Avoid copying large passages from source documentation.

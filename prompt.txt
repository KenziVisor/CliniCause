# Scientific positioning

## Primary claim

State confidently that CliniCause constructs two reusable, validated causal-analysis datasets from:

* MIMIC-III;
* PhysioNet 2012.

The resources preserve dataset-specific semantics through a common analytical interface.

## Meaning of “validated”

Define validation positively and concretely through four dimensions:

1. **Structural integrity**

   * expected files and schemas;
   * canonical identifiers;
   * expected columns and value contracts;
   * complete output sets.

2. **Cohort integrity**

   * identifier preservation;
   * exact equality at required handoffs;
   * rejection of silent intersections;
   * rejection of silent row shrinkage;
   * explicit mismatch failures.

3. **Artifact and provenance integrity**

   * manifests;
   * receipts;
   * fingerprints or hashes where supported;
   * prediction and checkpoint metadata;
   * voter sidecars;
   * run-scoped outputs;
   * stale-artifact and reuse checks.

4. **Analytical and empirical validation**

   * estimator-ready schemas;
   * predictive characterization;
   * application of multiple effect estimators;
   * matching and robustness diagnostics;
   * execution across two heterogeneous ICU sources.

End the validation subsection with one clear scope sentence close to:

> Validation establishes the structural integrity, cohort consistency, provenance, and analytical reuse of the constructed resources; clinical construct validity and causal identification remain separate scientific questions.

After defining this once, use the phrase `validated causal-analysis datasets` normally. Do not repeatedly apologize for it.

# Required subsection content

## 3.1 Source Cohorts and Analysis Units

Explain:

* MIMIC-III and PhysioNet 2012 as heterogeneous ICU sources;
* source access and high-level dataset roles;
* the primary analytical unit;
* in-hospital mortality outcome;
* source-specific covariates and data contracts;
* final admitted primary causal-analysis populations:

  * MIMIC-III: `26,845`;
  * PhysioNet 2012: `7,993`;
* why resources and results remain separate rather than pooled.

State confidently that the shared construction workflow preserves source-specific semantics.

Do not infer unverified temporal windows, raw cohort sizes, or historical producing settings.

## 3.2 Proxy-State Design and Deterministic Construction

Explain:

* the design-time LLM proposal process;
* project/human selection;
* deterministic source-coded construction;
* dataset-specific proxy ontologies;
* missing or unavailable measurement handling;
* canonical proxy-state fields;
* the role of proxy states as explicit analytical constructs.

The paper must make clear that proxy states provide a transparent interface between irregular records, prediction, and downstream analysis.

Do not describe them as chart-adjudicated diagnoses.

Do not over-expand prompt history; detailed prompt artifacts belong in the supplement.

## 3.3 Predictive Annotations from Irregular Time Series

Explain:

* multi-label proxy-state prediction;
* four learned models:

  * STraTS;
  * GRU;
  * GRU-D;
  * TCN;
* irregular-time-series input representation at the level needed for this paper;
* probabilities and thresholded binary predictions;
* normalized exports;
* archived aggregation of:

  * one deterministic rule-derived source;
  * four model-derived prediction sources;
* how the aggregate becomes an analytical proxy-state exposure layer.

Do not report predictive performance numbers here; those belong in Results.

Do not imply that prediction validates the clinical constructs.

## 3.4 Causal-Analysis Schema and DAG Metadata

Define what the constructed resource contains, including where supported:

* canonical record identifier;
* proxy-state exposure fields;
* in-hospital mortality outcome;
* observed covariates;
* dataset identifier;
* sampling condition;
* DAG or graph provenance;
* exposure-specific adjustment-set metadata;
* estimator-ready tables;
* run or artifact provenance identifiers.

Explain that source-coded DAGs operationalize explicit project assumptions and make adjustment choices inspectable and replaceable in follow-up work.

Emphasize reuse:

* researchers can test alternative estimators;
* researchers can refine proxy definitions;
* researchers can replace DAGs or adjustment sets;
* researchers can investigate assumptions without rebuilding the complete source-integration pipeline.

Do not claim that the project DAGs are clinically validated.

## 3.5 Dataset Validation and Provenance

This is the central contribution subsection.

Present the validation framework as an achieved engineering and research result.

Cover:

* structural validation;
* identifier and cohort contracts;
* malformed-input rejection;
* duplicate-conflict behavior;
* complete-output validation;
* prediction and voter metadata;
* manifests, receipts, and fingerprints;
* dataset-isolated run paths;
* deterministic or seed-controlled behavior where supported;
* current evidence boundaries between:

  * archived results;
  * historical producing code;
  * current repaired implementation;
  * runtime-validated execution.

Do not turn the subsection into a debugging history.

Describe the validation contracts that make the resource reusable, not the chronology of bugs that motivated them.

At minimum include claims for:

## Primary resource claims

* CliniCause constructs two reusable causal-analysis datasets.
* The resources are derived separately from MIMIC-III and PhysioNet 2012.
* Each resource includes explicit analysis identifiers, proxy-state exposures, mortality outcomes, observed covariates, graph-derived adjustment metadata, predictive annotations, and estimator-ready outputs.
* The resources preserve dataset-specific semantics through a common analytical interface.
* Validation supports structural integrity, cohort integrity, artifact integrity, and analytical reuse.

## Pipeline claims

* The pipeline is evidence tracked.
* Proxy-state design proposals were LLM assisted.
* Project-selected proposals were encoded in deterministic source.
* The LLM was not a runtime patient-level estimator.
* Four predictive models supplied learned annotations.
* Archived causal runs aggregate one rule-derived source and four prediction-derived sources.
* Source-coded DAGs operationalize exposure-specific adjustment assumptions.

## Empirical claims

* The resources contain 26,845 MIMIC-III and 7,993 PhysioNet primary causal-analysis records.
* STraTS leads the four archived MIMIC-III predictive metrics.
* GRU-D leads the corresponding PhysioNet metrics.
* CausalForestDML and LinearDML agree in direction for 19/19 comparisons.
* CausalPFN joins the prevailing direction in 18/19 comparisons.
* CausalPFN therefore provides meaningful cross-estimator triangulation.
* PhysioNet shock is the central estimator-disagreement case.
* The shared workflow is demonstrated across two heterogeneous ICU sources without numerical pooling.

## Reuse claims

* The resources support follow-up evaluation of alternative estimators.
* They support replacement or refinement of proxy definitions.
* They support evaluation of alternative DAGs and adjustment sets.
* They reduce the need to rebuild the complete integration pipeline for each follow-up study.
* They expose construct and cohort lineage for audit and extension.

For every claim, prefer direct positive wording. Add qualifications only where the evidence boundary requires them.

Do not weaken supported claims by beginning them with phrases such as:

```text

we just
preliminary
tentative
```

unless the evidence truly requires such wording.

# Introduction

Replace the Introduction placeholder with a result-aware, resource-led Introduction.

## Paragraph structure

Target five compact paragraphs.

### Paragraph 1 — Problem and opportunity

Establish:

* irregular ICU records are heterogeneous, sparse, and difficult to reuse;
* causal analysis requires more than prediction-ready time series;
* researchers need explicit exposure, outcome, covariate, graph, cohort, and provenance interfaces;
* reconstructing these interfaces repeatedly creates a barrier to reproducible causal AI research.

Lead with the research problem, not repository implementation details.

### Paragraph 2 — Gap

Explain that existing strands address:

* ICU time-series modeling;
* proxy or weak labels;
* observational causal estimation;
* clinical data resources;

but do not directly solve the integrated resource-construction problem addressed here.

The gap must be consistent with Related Work and should not rely on unsupported universal novelty language.

### Paragraph 3 — CliniCause approach

Introduce CliniCause clearly:

* constructs two validated causal-analysis datasets from MIMIC-III and PhysioNet 2012;
* retains dataset-specific semantics under a common analytical interface;
* combines deterministic proxy rules with predictive annotations from STraTS, GRU, GRU-D, and TCN;
* packages proxy exposures, mortality, observed covariates, DAG metadata, adjustment sets, and provenance;
* uses a structured design-time LLM protocol whose selected proposals are encoded into deterministic source artifacts.

Define “validated causal-analysis datasets” concisely:

* validated construction and analytical integrity;
* not validated causal identification.

Do not repeat the full four-part validation taxonomy from Section 3.

### Paragraph 4 — Headline empirical findings

State the most reviewer-relevant results:

* 26,845 MIMIC-III analysis records and nine admitted exposures;
* 7,993 PhysioNet analysis records and ten admitted exposures;
* STraTS leads all four archived MIMIC-III predictive metrics;
* GRU-D leads all four corresponding PhysioNet metrics;
* CausalForestDML and LinearDML agree in direction in 19/19 comparisons;
* all three estimators including CausalPFN agree in 18/19;
* broad `55/57` original-versus-downsampled directional preservation.

Keep this paragraph compact.

Mention PhysioNet shock only if needed to avoid making `18/19` abstract; do not narrate the full exception.

Use the exact terminology and evidentiary boundaries frozen in P5.

Do not claim:

* significance;
* clinical-label validity;
* causal identification;
* equal estimator magnitudes;
* randomized treatment effects;
* public availability.

### Paragraph 5 — Explicit contributions

Provide three or four numbered or sentence-level contributions.

Preferred four-contribution structure:

1. **Resource contribution:** two reusable, validated causal-analysis datasets spanning MIMIC-III and PhysioNet 2012.
2. **Construction contribution:** an evidence-tracked pipeline integrating proxy-state design, temporal prediction, DAG metadata, validation, and provenance.
3. **Empirical contribution:** full predictive and multi-estimator characterization, including `19/19` DML and `18/19` all-estimator directional agreement.
4. **Reuse contribution:** explicit interfaces enabling alternative proxy definitions, temporal models, DAGs, adjustment sets, causal estimators, and robustness studies.

The LLM should appear inside the construction contribution, not as a separate primary contribution.

End with a concise paper roadmap only if it materially improves navigation and fits the page budget.

## 5.1 Units of Analysis and Data Objects

Define the primary operational study unit carefully.

The text should state whether the repository operates on:

* ICU stays;
* patient records;
* time-series records;
* or another verified unit.

Where datasets or repositories use different source identifiers, explain that the implementation normalizes them to:

```text
ts_id
```

Do not claim that `ts_id` necessarily represents a globally unique person across multiple admissions unless that is explicitly established.

Define an irregular multivariate time series using notation consistent with:

```text
thesis-writing/planning/terminology_and_notation.md
```

A possible representation is an observation tuple:

```text
(i, t, v, x)
```

where:

* `i` identifies the study unit;
* `t` is elapsed time from admission or record start;
* `v` identifies the measured variable;
* `x` is the observed value.

Use the approved notation when it differs.

Explain that:

* observations occur at nonuniform times;
* not every variable is measured at every time;
* measurement frequencies differ across variables and records;
* a missing measurement is not automatically equivalent to a normal value.

Treat informative measurement or missingness processes as possible concerns rather than established causal findings.

---

## 5.2 Outcomes, Proxy States, and Analytical Tasks

Define the patient-level outcome used by the downstream causal repository:

```text
in_hospital_mortality
```

Describe it according to the verified processed-data contract.

Define the clinical state columns using approved terminology such as:

* derived proxy states;
* rule-based proxy labels;
* clinically inspired proxy phenotypes;
* weak clinical labels.

Explicitly state that they are not:

* verified diagnoses;
* manually adjudicated ground truth;
* confirmed physiological states;
* validated clinical phenotypes unless separate evidence exists.

At a high level, distinguish these tasks:

1. **Rule-based proxy-state construction**
   Binary proxy labels are derived from observed clinical data.

2. **Multi-label proxy-state prediction**
   Predictive models estimate the rule-derived proxy labels from irregular time-series inputs.

3. **Proxy-label aggregation**
   Binary outputs from multiple predictive models may be normalized or combined.

4. **Mortality prediction or association analysis**
   Proxy-state representations are assessed for prognostic or associative information.

5. **Observational causal-effect estimation**
   Selected proxy-state indicators are modeled as exposures, with adjusted contrasts estimated under explicit assumptions.

Do not describe the detailed implementation of these tasks here.

---

## 5.3 Study Design and Interpretation Boundaries

Describe the work as a retrospective observational analysis of ICU time-series datasets.

State clearly that:

* no intervention is assigned by the study;
* proxy-state variables are analytical constructs;
* software parameters named `treatment` may represent modeled binary exposures rather than administered therapies;
* later causal interpretation depends on assumptions concerning the DAG, adjustment set, overlap, consistency, and unmeasured confounding;
* these assumptions are not proven by the existence of the implementation.

Prefer wording such as:

```text
exposure
modeled treatment variable
proxy-state exposure
adjusted effect estimate
conditional causal interpretation
```

Avoid clinical-actionability language.

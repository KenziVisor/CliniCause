# 9. Source-Role Audit

For every cited claim, classify the source role:

```text
PRIMARY_DATASET
PRIMARY_METHOD
PRIMARY_CLINICAL_DEFINITION
CLINICAL_GUIDELINE_OR_CONSENSUS
SOFTWARE_IMPLEMENTATION
REVIEW_OR_SURVEY
APPLICATION_EXAMPLE
OPTIONAL_BACKGROUND
```

Verify the following boundaries.

## 9.1 Dataset sources

Dataset publications may support:

* dataset scope;
* challenge or database context;
* access model;
* published benchmark framing.

They may not support:

* local preprocessing execution;
* local cohort counts;
* local split construction;
* local artifact lineage;
* local data quality.

## 9.2 Predictive method sources

Method papers may support:

* canonical architecture;
* representation;
* loss or pretraining concept;
* method-level assumptions and limitations.

They may not support:

* local implementation equivalence;
* local performance;
* local checkpoint selection;
* local prediction-export provenance.

## 9.3 Clinical sources

Clinical definitions may support:

* accepted syndrome concepts;
* measurement domains;
* high-level clinical rationale.

They may not automatically support:

* every project threshold;
* complete implementation of the source definition;
* diagnosis claims;
* proxy-state validation;
* transport across datasets.

## 9.4 Causal-method sources

Causal papers may support:

* identification concepts;
* theoretical estimator properties;
* overlap and sensitivity concepts;
* HTE framing.

They may not support:

* correctness of the project DAG;
* absence of unmeasured confounding;
* local estimator execution;
* local estimate validity;
* clinical actionability.

## 9.5 Software sources

Software papers may support:

* library scope;
* implementation context;
* available workflow concepts.

They must not replace the primary mathematical method source when one exists.
